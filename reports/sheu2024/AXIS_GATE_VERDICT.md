### Composite: **RED — but driven by statistical underpowering, not biology**

Every q-value is exactly 1.0 across all axes and all seeds. With effectively
n = 3 informative train pseudo-donors per cytokine (M0_rep1, M0_rep2, M1_IFNg_rep1;
the other two pseudo-donors contribute only 0hr Unstim cells), the one-sided
Wilcoxon signed-rank test has minimum achievable p ≈ 1/8 = 0.125 even in the
noiseless limit. After Bonferroni × 4 cell types (`mac_c0…mac_c3`) and BH-FDR
across the 49 ordered pairs (7 active stimuli + PBS minus self-pairs), no axis
can clear α = 0.05 regardless of effect size. The RED is therefore an
identifiability failure of the §21 NHST gate at this donor count, not evidence
that the cascade signal is absent. The Spearman ρ across seeds is `nan` for
the same reason (all axis-ranking scores collapse to −log10(1) = 0).

→ Cascade signal not recoverable **by the §21 Wilcoxon gate** from 3hr BMDM
at n = 3 effective pseudo-donors. Before reconsidering phase 2, alternative
read-outs that don't require donor-level Wilcoxon power should be explored:
(1) inspect raw per-donor bias `b_per_donor` magnitudes and sign-agreement
across the 3 donors, (2) try `direction_mode: cell_type` and relax the
Bonferroni-over-T correction (no relay search), (3) consider a 1hr or 5hr
time-point subset if donor coverage there is higher.
