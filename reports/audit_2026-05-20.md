# Project Audit — 2026-05-20

## 1. Headline

The pipeline **does what the spec says.** Three parallel agents audited the
codebase against `CLAUDE.md` §5–20 and the active cascade-detection pipeline
end-to-end. **No critical correctness bugs were found.** The 0-reproducible-
triples result from the recent ablation_union chain is a **semantic
strictness problem in `report_cascade_triples.py`**, not a silent
computational error.

There is, however, a real **codebase hygiene problem**: ~24 scripts +
matching SLURM wrappers are unreachable from any active submit chain. They
were paired with `run_*.slurm` files that nothing auto-submits, and they are
safe to remove pending a sanity check against the user's local notes.

The remainder of this document is the evidence behind those two claims.

## 5. The 0-triples diagnosis

`scripts/report_cascade_triples.py:155–175` enforces the following
conjunction per seed before counting a triple as reportable:

1. Geo: `cascade_call[(A,B)] == "A->B"` (line 157)
2. Geo: `p_fwd_bonf[(A,B,T)] ≤ α` (collected in `_geo_significant_T`)
3. Ablation: `direction_call == "A→B"` (line 167)
4. Ablation: `T = argmax_T mean_pooled_relay`, `relay > 0` (line 169)
5. **Cross-method T-agreement: `abl_T ∈ geo_T_map`** (line 174)

The result on the live run was:

- 18 seed-level triples passed all five clauses across the 8 seeds.
- Maximum per-pair reproducibility was **2/8 seeds**, so 0 survived the ≥5/8 filter.
- None of the 11 KNOWN_CASCADES appeared even at the per-seed level.

The session summary's **finding X3** ("direction information from geo is
unreliable; asymmetry between (A→B) and (B→A) geo scores reflects which
cytokine has the larger response, not the direction of the cascade") makes
this expected: geo's `cascade_call` flips around across seeds for the same
ordered pair, so the intersection
`{seeds where geo says A→B} ∩ {seeds where ablation says A→B}` is small,
and the further constraint that they agree on **T as well** collapses it.

This is exactly the relaxation queued for Phase 2 of the active plan (a
`--mode` flag on `report_cascade_triples.py`). No code change is applied in
this audit document.
